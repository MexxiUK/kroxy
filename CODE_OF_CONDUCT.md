# Code of Conduct

## The Short Version

**All contributions are welcome. Be nice to each other.**

That's it. We're building something useful together, and we want this to be a place where everyone feels comfortable contributing.

## The Slightly Longer Version

We welcome contributions from everyone, regardless of experience level, background, or how long you've been coding. Whether this is your first open source contribution or your thousandth, we're glad you're here.

**What we expect:**
- Treat people with respect
- Be patient with newcomers
- Give constructive feedback
- Accept feedback gracefully
- Focus on the code, not the person

**What we won't tolerate:**
- Harassment or discrimination
- Personal attacks
- Trolling or being deliberately difficult
- Publishing others' private information
- Behavior that makes others uncomfortable

## If Something Goes Wrong

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, email us at security@kroxy.dev. We'll take it seriously and handle it fairly.

We may issue warnings, temporary bans, or permanent bans depending on the situation. We'd rather not, but we will if needed to keep the community welcoming.

## Why This Matters

Open source works because people contribute their time and effort freely. That only happens when people feel safe and welcome. We're committed to making this a place where that happens.

---

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other project-related communication.
